Color contrast checker

WCAG 2.1 · in-browser ← free tools · the arithmetic
Whether text is readable shouldn't be a paid feature. Accessible color contrast is a fixed, published formula (WCAG 2.1) — yet plenty of design suites gate the checker behind a subscription. This one computes the exact ratio on your device and tells you, plainly, whether your colors pass. Why this exists →

Colors

Ratio = (Llight + 0.05) / (Ldark + 0.05), where L is sRGB relative luminance. Large text = 18.66px bold or 24px regular and up. Range is 1:1 (identical) to 21:1 (black on white).
